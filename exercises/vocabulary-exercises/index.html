    Vocabulary Exercises¶

    Purpose¶

    Technical English depends heavily on lexical precision. In engineering communication, an incorrect or imprecise word can create confusion, reduce credibility, or produce operational ambiguity. The goal of this page is to help the learner build controlled, reusable, and context-aware technical vocabulary.

    The exercises below focus not only on knowing a term, but on understanding: - what it means; - where it is used; - what other terms it is commonly associated with; - what wording should be avoided.

    Exercise 1 — Matching Terms to Definitions¶

    Match each term to the most appropriate definition.

    Terms¶

    1. enclosure
    2. interface
    3. bracket
    4. threshold
    5. wear
    6. redundancy
    7. alignment
    8. compliance

    Definitions¶

    A. the condition of meeting a requirement, rule, or specification
    B. the point at which a reaction, change, or function is triggered
    C. progressive damage caused by use, friction, or exposure
    D. a structural support used for mounting or securing a part
    E. a protective outer casing or housing
    F. the arrangement that allows two systems or parts to connect or interact
    G. the correct relative positioning of components
    H. the duplication of functions or elements to improve reliability

    Follow-Up¶

    Write one sentence for each term in a realistic engineering context.

    Exercise 2 — Classifying Vocabulary by Function¶

    Place the following terms in the correct category: - inspect - withstand - connector - enclosure - reliable - verify - degradation - interface - compliant - calibrate - fault - robust

    Categories¶

    • nouns for components
    • nouns for conditions or states
    • verbs for technical actions
    • adjectives for evaluation and description

    Reflection Task¶

    Explain why knowing the grammatical category of a technical term helps writing quality.

    Exercise 3 — Stronger Technical Alternatives¶

    Replace the weak wording with a more professional technical term or expression.

    1. The part broke.
    2. The system works well.
    3. The cable moved.
    4. The device got hot.
    5. The result was bad.
    6. The component is very strong.
    7. The technician looked at the unit.
    8. The signal disappeared.

    Example¶

    Weak: The part broke.
    Possible stronger wording: The bracket fractured under load. / The component failed during testing.

    Reviewer Focus¶

    More than one answer may be acceptable, provided the wording is more precise and technically plausible.

    Exercise 4 — Collocation Awareness¶

    Complete the collocations with the most suitable word.

    1. perform a ______ inspection
    2. meet the ______ criteria
    3. restore normal ______
    4. detect a ______ condition
    5. withstand mechanical ______
    6. maintain structural ______
    7. issue a ______ report
    8. ensure proper ______

    Possible words¶

    operation, alignment, acceptance, vibration, scheduled, integrity, fault, technical

    Follow-Up¶

    Write a short paragraph using at least four of the completed collocations.

    Exercise 5 — Near-Synonym Control¶

    Explain the difference between the following pairs: 1. fault / failure
    2. reliable / robust
    3. test / inspection
    4. issue / anomaly
    5. operate / function
    6. repair / replace

    Guidance¶

    Do not translate only. Explain the likely difference in engineering use.

    Exercise 6 — Vocabulary in Context¶

    Complete the paragraph with suitable technical vocabulary.

    During routine __, the maintenance team identified abnormal _ on the cable support bracket. Although the unit remained fully , the affected part was removed and replaced as a precautionary measure. A follow-up confirmed correct installation and restored ___.

    Suggested term bank¶

    alignment, operational, wear, inspection, function, interface, verification, compliant

    Exercise 7 — Civil vs Defence Vocabulary Awareness¶

    For each pair below, explain whether the terms are equivalent, partially overlapping, or context-dependent.

    1. equipment / asset
    2. user / operator
    3. failure / loss of capability
    4. maintenance / sustainment
    5. component / item
    6. testing / validation

    Task¶

    Write a short note explaining why terminology may change depending on sector, audience, and document purpose.

    Exercise 8 — Adjective Quality Control¶

    Choose the better adjective for each sentence and justify your choice.

    1. The enclosure is strong / impact-resistant.
    2. The interface is good / reliable.
    3. The design is simple / maintainable.
    4. The result is positive / compliant.
    5. The system is modern / interoperable.

    Reviewer Focus¶

    The learner should prefer adjectives that carry technical meaning over adjectives that are merely positive.

    Exercise 9 — Build a Mini Glossary¶

    Create a mini glossary of ten terms related to one of the topics below: - maintenance; - safety; - systems integration; - testing; - documentation control.

    For each term, include: - the term; - part of speech; - short definition; - one example sentence.

    Exercise 10 — Vocabulary for Reuse¶

    Choose five terms from this page and turn them into a reusable phrasebank section for the repository. Your output should include: - the term; - a common collocation; - an example sentence; - a note on typical misuse.

    Output Goal¶

    This exercise is designed to produce content that can later be moved to: - a glossary page; - a language-tools page; - a quality-assurance note.
