    Grammar Exercises¶

    Purpose¶

    Grammar in technical English should not be treated as an abstract system detached from professional use. In engineering communication, grammar controls precision, hierarchy, obligation, sequence, traceability, and cause-effect relations. The aim of this page is to reinforce grammar where it matters most: in descriptions, reports, requirements, procedures, and technical evaluations.

    Exercise 1 — Present Simple for Technical Function¶

    Complete the sentences with the correct verb form.

    1. The sensor module ______ temperature deviations in real time.
    2. The control unit ______ status data from the remote terminal.
    3. The enclosure ______ the internal electronics from dust ingress.
    4. The interface cable ______ power and signal traffic simultaneously.
    5. The software routine ______ diagnostic checks during startup.

    Follow-Up¶

    Write five additional present simple sentences describing the function of a system or subsystem.

    Exercise 2 — Passive Voice in Technical Description¶

    Rewrite the sentences using the passive voice where appropriate.

    1. The technician secures the module with four mounting screws.
    2. The test team performed the calibration before the environmental trial.
    3. The maintenance staff replaced the damaged connector.
    4. The design team integrated an additional protection layer.
    5. The operator initiates the verification sequence from the main panel.

    Review Focus¶

    The learner should understand that the passive voice is often used when the action matters more than the actor, but it should not be overused.

    Exercise 3 — Shall, Must, Should, May¶

    Choose the most suitable modal verb.

    1. The unit ______ withstand vibration levels defined in the specification.
    2. The enclosure ______ be opened while the power supply is connected.
    3. The technician ______ verify alignment before final tightening.
    4. Alternative materials ______ be used subject to approval.
    5. The event log ______ be retained for subsequent analysis.

    Reflection¶

    Explain why each modal verb is appropriate in its sentence.

    Exercise 4 — Conditional Logic in Procedures¶

    Complete the procedural statements.

    1. If the system __ (detect) an internal fault, the alarm sequence ____ (start) automatically.
    2. If the connector ______ (be) not fully seated, communication instability may occur.
    3. If abnormal vibration __ (persist), the unit should ____ (remove) from service.
    4. If the test result __ (fall) outside the specified range, the sample shall ____ (retest).

    Follow-Up¶

    Write two additional conditional sentences suitable for a maintenance or safety procedure.

    Exercise 5 — Relative Clauses for Precision¶

    Combine the sentences into one more precise sentence using a relative clause.

    1. The cable was replaced. The cable showed visible insulation damage.
    2. The module is mounted on the rear panel. The module controls the acquisition sequence.
    3. The procedure was revised. The procedure previously allowed incomplete documentation.
    4. The test article failed during vibration exposure. The test article had passed the electrical check.

    Reviewer Focus¶

    Relative clauses are used to reduce fragmentation and improve technical precision.

    Exercise 6 — Sequencing and Process Grammar¶

    Reorder the steps and rewrite them as a coherent process paragraph: - perform the final inspection; - disconnect the power source; - open the access panel; - replace the damaged connector; - verify the interface alignment; - restore power and conduct a function check.

    Output Requirement¶

    Use sequencing language such as: - first, - prior to, - once completed, - subsequently, - after verification, - finally.

    Exercise 7 — Error Correction¶

    Correct the grammatical and stylistic problems in the paragraph below.

    The unit have been installed on the left support bracket and are connected to a 24 V source. If abnormal noise will occur, the operator must to stop the system and should checks the mounting points. The damaged parts was replaced and the operation restore after the inspection.

    Task¶

    Rewrite the paragraph in correct professional English.

    Exercise 8 — Nominal vs Verbal Style¶

    Rewrite the sentences in a more natural technical style.

    1. The performance of an inspection of the cable routing was carried out.
    2. The execution of calibration was completed before testing.
    3. A verification of the alignment of the bracket was performed.
    4. The implementation of the update improved stability.

    Reviewer Focus¶

    The learner should avoid excessive nominalization where a more direct verb structure improves clarity.

    Exercise 9 — Requirement Rewriting¶

    Rewrite the following informal instructions as formal requirement statements.

    1. The battery has to last at least ten hours.
    2. You should protect the cable from abrasion.
    3. It is allowed to use another material if approved.
    4. Don’t use the unit outside the specified temperature range.

    Suggested Output Style¶

    Use a controlled specification tone.

    Exercise 10 — Grammar for Documentation Reuse¶

    Write a short paragraph of 90 to 120 words describing a subsystem. Your paragraph must include: - at least two present simple sentences; - one passive construction; - one requirement sentence using shall or must; - one conditional sentence; - one relative clause.

    Output Goal¶

    The paragraph should be suitable for later reuse in: - a module page; - a system-description template; - a review-playbook example.
