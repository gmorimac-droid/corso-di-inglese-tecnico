    Oral Communication Tasks¶

    Purpose¶

    Engineering professionals often need to communicate orally in situations that require precision, concision, and controlled terminology. Typical situations include briefings, design reviews, handovers, maintenance updates, test summaries, and technical coordination meetings.

    The purpose of this page is not to train casual conversation. It is to train spoken professional English for technical communication.

    Task 1 — System Briefing¶

    Prepare a two-minute briefing on one of the following: - monitoring unit; - power distribution unit; - sensor module; - actuator assembly.

    Required Content¶

    • overall purpose;
    • main functions;
    • interfaces;
    • one limitation or operational consideration;
    • one maintenance consideration.

    Reviewer Focus¶

    The briefing should be clear, structured, and terminology-controlled.

    Task 2 — Test Update¶

    You are asked to update the team after an environmental test.

    Notes¶

    • unit remained operational;
    • response time increased at upper temperature limit;
    • no critical failure;
    • minor wear observed on cable bracket;
    • endurance testing still required.

    Task¶

    Deliver a spoken update of 60 to 90 seconds.

    Suggested Structure¶

    • test objective;
    • main result;
    • observed issue;
    • recommended next step.

    Task 3 — Fault Explanation¶

    Explain the following fault case orally: - intermittent communication loss; - initial checks performed; - loose connector identified; - connector re-secured; - normal communication restored.

    Additional Requirement¶

    End with one preventive recommendation.

    Task 4 — Meeting Contribution¶

    Imagine you are in a design review meeting. You need to comment on the revised bracket design.

    Known Points¶

    • improved maintainability;
    • simplified cable routing;
    • larger enclosure depth;
    • tighter alignment requirement during installation.

    Task¶

    State your evaluation in 5 to 7 sentences.

    Task 5 — Asking for Clarification¶

    Prepare three oral clarification questions for each topic: 1. unclear revision status of a document; 2. missing test limit in procedure; 3. ambiguous interface requirement; 4. unexplained anomaly in report.

    Reviewer Focus¶

    Questions should be precise and professional.

    Task 6 — Maintenance Handover¶

    Simulate a maintenance handover at the end of a shift.

    Situation¶

    • bracket replaced;
    • alignment verified;
    • function test passed;
    • follow-up inspection still required after 100 hours.

    Task¶

    Deliver a concise spoken handover of about one minute.

    Task 7 — Safety Brief¶

    Prepare a short safety briefing before maintenance activity involving enclosure access.

    Mandatory Points¶

    • power isolation;
    • residual hazard awareness;
    • verification before intervention;
    • documentation update after completion.

    Task 8 — Civil vs Defence Register Awareness¶

    Explain the same system in two oral registers: - version A for a general industrial engineering audience; - version B for a defence-oriented programme review.

    Reflection¶

    Identify how terminology changes while maintaining professional neutrality.

    Task 9 — Oral Summary from Notes¶

    Convert the notes below into a short oral summary: - revised interface layout; - reduced routing complexity; - no major issue in baseline validation; - detailed fatigue assessment pending.

    Target¶

    30 to 45 seconds.

    Task 10 — Repository-Ready Speaking Script¶

    Write a spoken script that could later be stored in the repository as a model briefing. Choose one topic: - test status update; - maintenance handover; - system overview; - anomaly explanation; - safety opening statement.

    Length¶

    150 to 220 words.

    Output Goal¶

    The script should be suitable for: - oral rehearsal; - teaching demonstration; - adaptation into a briefing template.
