    Quality Assurance¶

    Purpose of This Section¶

    The purpose of the Quality Assurance section is to define the editorial, linguistic, structural, and maintenance rules that govern the repository. In a documentation-driven course, quality assurance is not an optional final check carried out shortly before publication. It is a continuous discipline that influences how pages are planned, written, reviewed, linked, revised, and released.

    This section therefore acts as the operational control layer of the project. It translates general intentions such as clarity, consistency, professional tone, and usability into concrete review criteria.

    In practical terms, the pages in this section help the contributor answer questions such as the following:

    • Is this page written in a stable and professional register?
    • Does the terminology match the glossaries and modules already published?
    • Is the page architecturally coherent with the rest of the repository?
    • Are the internal links useful and intentional rather than decorative?
    • Is the page mature enough to be considered publication-ready?

    Scope¶

    The quality assurance framework applies to all content types in the repository, including:

    • course overview pages;
    • module pages;
    • glossaries;
    • language tools;
    • exercises;
    • templates;
    • civil vs defence comparison pages;
    • final project pages;
    • future appendices and release notes.

    Guiding Principle¶

    The repository should be treated as a professional documentation environment rather than as a loose collection of teaching notes. As a consequence, every page should be evaluated not only for grammatical correctness, but also for:

    • information value;
    • readability;
    • structural integrity;
    • terminology governance;
    • documentation usefulness;
    • long-term maintainability.

    Pages in This Section¶

    Recommended Use¶

    A practical workflow is the following:

    1. Draft the page.
    2. Check structure against the page architecture checklist.
    3. Verify terminology against the glossary governance rules.
    4. Add and review links according to the cross-linking strategy.
    5. Align wording and tone with the editorial style guide.
    6. Perform a final publication check with the release readiness checklist.

    Quality Assurance as a Teaching Tool¶

    This section also has a pedagogical role. Learners working at B2 level often believe that technical writing is mainly a matter of translating vocabulary from Italian into English. In reality, professional documentation quality depends on a broader set of abilities:

    • selecting relevant information;
    • reducing ambiguity;
    • distinguishing fact from interpretation;
    • structuring content in usable blocks;
    • using disciplined terminology;
    • maintaining consistency across multiple pages.

    For that reason, the quality assurance section should be used not only during final review, but also during drafting, revision, and teacher feedback.
