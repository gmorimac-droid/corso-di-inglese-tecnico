# Glossaries

## What This Section Contains

This section provides the core terminology support for the course.

The glossaries are designed to help learners:
- understand technical terms more accurately;
- use vocabulary more consistently;
- avoid vague or overly general wording;
- build stronger technical writing and speaking habits.

The goal is not to collect as many words as possible. The goal is to support **clear, controlled, and reusable technical English**.

## At a Glance

The glossary section includes:

- **core vocabulary** used across engineering contexts;
- **discipline-oriented terminology** for specific technical areas;
- **context-specific terminology** for regulated, institutional, and defence-oriented settings.

## How to Use the Glossaries

A practical way to use each glossary page is:

1. read the definition;
2. study the example sentence;
3. check the usage note;
4. compare the term with related or similar terms;
5. reuse it in your own sentence or document.

## What Each Entry Typically Includes

Most glossary entries include:

- **Definition**  
  to explain the meaning of the term clearly;

- **Example**  
  to show how the term is used in context;

- **Usage note**  
  to explain where the term is especially useful;

- **Learner note or contrast note**  
  to help avoid common mistakes;

- **Related terms**  
  to support vocabulary expansion and consistency.

## Practical Advice

Do not memorise isolated words.

A better method is to learn:
- the term;
- a typical collocation;
- a sentence pattern;
- the kind of document where the term is commonly used.

For example, it is more useful to learn:
- *comply with a requirement*
- *detect a fault condition*
- *issue a revised document*

than to memorise only:
- *comply*
- *detect*
- *issue*

## Final Note

A strong glossary is not the longest glossary.

A strong glossary is one that helps the learner choose the right term more quickly, more consistently, and with less ambiguity.
