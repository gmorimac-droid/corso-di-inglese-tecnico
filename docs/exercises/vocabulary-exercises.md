# Vocabulary Exercises

## Purpose

Technical English depends heavily on lexical precision. In engineering communication, an incorrect or imprecise word can create confusion, reduce credibility, or produce operational ambiguity. The goal of this page is to help the learner build controlled, reusable, and context-aware technical vocabulary.

The exercises below focus not only on knowing a term, but on understanding:
- what it means;
- where it is used;
- what other terms it is commonly associated with;
- what wording should be avoided.

## Exercise 3 — Stronger Technical Alternatives

Replace the weak wording with a more professional technical term or expression.

1. The part broke.  
2. The system works well.  
3. The cable moved.  
4. The device got hot.  
5. The result was bad.  
6. The component is very strong.  
7. The technician looked at the unit.  
8. The signal disappeared.

### Example
Weak: *The part broke.*  
Possible stronger wording: *The bracket fractured under load.* / *The component failed during testing.*

### Reviewer Focus
More than one answer may be acceptable, provided the wording is more precise and technically plausible.
