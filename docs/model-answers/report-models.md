# Report Models

!!! note "How to read model answers"
    Model answers should be analysed, not memorised. Compare them against your own draft to identify improvements in structure, evidence handling, terminology, and documentary usefulness.

## Short technical report model

### Purpose

To show how a short engineering report can remain concise while preserving evidential discipline.

### Model answer

**Purpose.** This report summarises the inspection and retest activity performed on the lower connector interface of the bench-test assembly after intermittent startup signal loss was reported.

**Configuration background.** The activity was conducted on the current bench-test configuration using the installed Revision B enclosure and the standard communication harness.

**Activity performed.** Visual inspection, continuity verification, controlled connector handling, and repeated startup cycles were performed in sequence to establish whether the reported condition could be reproduced and localised.

**Findings.** No permanent cable discontinuity was detected during continuity verification. However, signal loss could be reproduced when minor lateral movement was introduced at the lower connector interface. Local inspection indicated reduced retention stability at the connector boundary.

**Limitation.** At the time of writing, the conclusion remains valid for the tested configuration only. Additional inspection is required to determine whether the same condition exists on related assemblies.

**Conclusion and recommended action.** The available evidence supports local connector retention degradation as the most probable cause of the intermittent signal-loss condition. Connector replacement and comparative inspection of similar assemblies are therefore recommended.

### Review commentary

The report works because each section has a single function. It avoids emotional language and does not claim more certainty than the evidence allows.
