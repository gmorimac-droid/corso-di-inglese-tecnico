# Safety and Incident Models

!!! note "How to read model answers"
    Model answers should be analysed, not memorised. Compare them against your own draft to identify improvements in structure, evidence handling, terminology, and documentary usefulness.

## Near-miss summary model

During routine bench handling, an operator initiated connector disengagement before the local power-isolation confirmation step had been verbally completed. No injury or equipment damage occurred. The activity was stopped immediately, the connector state was re-secured, and the isolation sequence was repeated under supervision. The event is classified as a near-miss because the unsafe condition was interrupted before exposure progressed to harm. Immediate corrective action includes reinforcement of the verbal confirmation step and review of the relevant bench-handling briefing note.

## Commentary

This answer remains neutral, avoids blame-based wording, and preserves the difference between event, consequence, and corrective response.

## Residual-risk wording model

Residual risk remains low provided that the revised handling sequence is applied consistently and that the power-isolation confirmation step is clearly acknowledged before interface access.
