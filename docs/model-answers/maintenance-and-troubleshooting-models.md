# Maintenance and Troubleshooting Models

!!! note "How to read model answers"
    Model answers should be analysed, not memorised. Compare them against your own draft to identify improvements in structure, evidence handling, terminology, and documentary usefulness.

## Maintenance log entry model

During scheduled inspection of the communication assembly, local contamination was observed around the lower connector interface. No mechanical damage or abnormal deformation was identified. The affected area was cleaned using the approved local cleaning method, after which continuity and startup verification were repeated. No further abnormal behaviour was observed during the immediate post-action check. The current release statement is limited to the inspected assembly and the verification sequence performed on the date of maintenance.

## Troubleshooting model

Intermittent startup signal loss was reported during bench verification. Initial continuity checks did not reveal permanent harness damage. The fault was subsequently reproduced under minor connector movement, indicating a probable retention-related interface issue. Connector replacement restored stable communication during repeated startup cycles. Further inspection of comparable units is recommended to determine whether the condition represents an isolated occurrence or a wider configuration risk.

## Premium distinction

The maintenance entry is **record-oriented**, while the troubleshooting note is **diagnostic-oriented**. Learners should understand that these document types can describe the same event with different priorities.
