?# Templates and Models

## What This Section Contains

This section provides reusable templates for common engineering communication tasks.

The templates are designed to help learners and professionals:
- organise information more clearly;
- use more controlled technical English;
- reduce uncertainty when drafting recurring documents;
- produce outputs that are easier to review and reuse.

The templates are not intended as rigid forms. They are practical starting points for structured professional writing.

## At a Glance

The templates in this section cover:

- technical emails;
- meeting notes;
- technical reports;
- incident and troubleshooting records;
- maintenance logs;
- system descriptions;
- test reports;
- requirement writing;
- briefing structures.

## How to Use the Templates

A template can be used in three main ways.

### 1. Training Use
Use the template as part of a guided exercise.

In this case, the main objective is to practise structure, wording, and documentary discipline.

### 2. Professional Simulation
Use the template to simulate a realistic engineering deliverable.

This is useful for:
- report writing;
- incident communication;
- system description;
- meeting documentation;
- briefing practice.

### 3. Repository Use
Adapt the template for use in the MkDocs project.

In this case, the template becomes part of the learner’s documentation framework and can support repeated outputs.

## Recommended Working Method

A simple workflow is:

1. identify the document type;
2. choose the closest template;
3. fill in the factual sections first;
4. write the descriptive or analytical sections next;
5. review terminology, clarity, and consistency;
6. adapt the result if the format will be reused.

## Review Questions

Before finalising a document based on a template, check the following:

- Is the purpose of the document clear?
- Is the level of detail appropriate?
- Is the wording clear and professionally neutral?
- Are facts, observations, and recommendations clearly separated?
- Is the structure easy to review and reuse?

## Why Templates Matter

In technical communication, many weak documents fail not because the information is missing, but because the structure is unstable.

A good template helps the writer:
- focus on the right information;
- organise it logically;
- maintain consistency across documents;
- reduce avoidable rewriting.
