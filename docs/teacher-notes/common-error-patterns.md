# Common Error Patterns

!!! note "Teaching use note"
    These notes are intended to stabilise correction behaviour across tasks, modules, and repository outputs. Use them to keep assessment principles explicit and repeatable.

## Italian-to-English transfer patterns

### Overlong sentence rhythm

Students may place too much information into one sentence with delayed logic. This often makes the paragraph difficult to scan.

### Soft, vague adjectival style

Examples include *good*, *adequate*, *serious*, *important*, *particular*, or *specific* without technical support.

### Literal translation of documentary verbs

Examples include:
- *make a control* instead of *perform a check* or *carry out an inspection*;
- *it is convenient to* instead of *it is advisable to* or *it is recommended to*;
- *signalation* instead of *reporting* or *notification*.

### Misuse of modal verbs

Confusion between *shall*, *should*, *must*, and *can* is common and can seriously weaken technical documentation.

### Premature causal claims

Students may jump from a symptom to a confirmed root cause with no evidence language in between.

## Teaching response

These errors should be corrected systematically and linked back to document function rather than treated as isolated language faults.
