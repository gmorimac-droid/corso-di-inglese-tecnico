## Who This Course Is For

This course is intended for learners who already have a general English level around **B1+/B2** and need stronger control of **technical and professional English** in engineering contexts.

It is designed for people who must not only understand technical English, but also use it in documentation, reporting, coordination, and structured communication.

## Primary Audience

The course is especially suitable for:

- engineering students;
- technical institute students;
- junior engineers;
- maintenance personnel;
- quality and compliance staff;
- documentation contributors;
- project support staff;
- professionals entering international technical environments.

## Secondary Audience

The course may also benefit professionals who already work in industry but need to improve:

- documentary clarity;
- terminology control;
- consistency in technical writing;
- confidence in English-language communication.

In this case, the course can also function as a **language standardisation framework** for teams that produce recurring technical content.

## Typical Learner Needs

A typical learner may need support in one or more of the following areas:

- reading technical manuals more efficiently;
- writing emails, notes, and reports more clearly;
- speaking during meetings, briefings, or design reviews;
- understanding maintenance and troubleshooting language;
- using safety and compliance terminology correctly;
- contributing to English-language documentation repositories.

## Sector Relevance

The course is particularly relevant in sectors where engineering language must remain clear, controlled, and professional, including:

- civil industry;
- regulated industrial operations;
- dual-use technology environments;
- defence-related industrial organisations.

The focus remains on **professional technical English**, not on specialist doctrine.

## What the Course Does Not Assume

The course does not assume:

- native-like fluency;
- advanced academic linguistics;
- deep specialisation in a single engineering discipline.

It starts from a practical B2-oriented foundation and develops the learner’s ability to operate credibly across different technical situations.

## In Practical Terms

This course is a good fit for learners who can already read some English, but want to move from:

- **partial understanding** to **controlled use**;
- **general English** to **technical English**;
- **classroom exercises** to **usable professional output**.
