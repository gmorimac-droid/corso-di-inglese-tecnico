# Phrasebank for Email Writing

## Purpose of this page

Technical emails are often short, but they still carry engineering risk. A poorly written email can create ambiguity about status, responsibility, requirement level, or revision state. In many organisations, emails also become part of the decision trail, especially during document review, issue resolution, and coordination between engineering, quality, procurement, and maintenance teams.

This page provides reusable patterns for emails that are concise, professional, and suitable for technical environments.

## 1. Opening with purpose

The opening of a technical email should tell the reader immediately why the message exists.

### Core phrases
- *Please find attached revision 2 of the test summary for your review.*
- *This message is to confirm the current status of the interface control draft.*
- *I am writing to request clarification regarding the acceptance wording in Section 6.*
- *The purpose of this email is to report an issue affecting document release.*
- *This note provides a short update on the current verification status.*
- *Please see below a summary of the open technical points discussed today.*

## 2. Referring to attachments and revisions

### Core phrases
- *The attached version supersedes the previous draft circulated on 12 May.*
- *The current revision incorporates the dimensional update discussed during the review.*
- *Section 4 has been updated to reflect the revised interface allocation.*
- *The main change affects the maintenance-access geometry and associated note text.*
- *For ease of review, the updated paragraphs are highlighted in yellow.*
- *The attached report includes the latest available test data.*

## 3. Requesting action

### Core phrases
- *Please review the attached file and return comments by Friday.*
- *Kindly confirm whether the revised connector layout is acceptable.*
- *Please advise whether the proposed wording is sufficient for internal issue.*
- *Your confirmation is requested before the document is released externally.*
- *Please confirm whether the change applies to all units or to the tested configuration only.*
- *Could you review the traceability statement in Section 5 and advise whether further clarification is required?*

### Usage note
A strong request identifies the object, the action, and the time frame. It should not force the recipient to infer what response is expected.

## 4. Reporting status by email

### Core phrases
- *The document is currently with quality for final review.*
- *The issue remains open pending environmental retest.*
- *The latest inspection did not reproduce the previously reported anomaly.*
- *Release has been placed on hold until the remaining comments are closed.*
- *The updated configuration is now available in the shared repository.*
- *No further technical concerns have been raised at this stage.*

## 5. Requesting clarification

### Core phrases
- *Please clarify whether the note in Section 3 is intended as a requirement or as guidance.*
- *Could you confirm the source of the dimensional value quoted in the draft?*
- *Please advise whether the acceptance criterion should remain unchanged for revision 4.*
- *It would be helpful to confirm whether the current wording reflects the agreed baseline.*

## 6. Escalating a concern professionally

### Core phrases
- *At present, the available evidence does not support release approval.*
- *Further clarification is required before this point can be considered closed.*
- *The issue may affect traceability and should therefore be reviewed before external issue.*
- *I would recommend holding the release until the requirement wording has been aligned.*
- *The current text appears broader than the evidence supports.*

## 7. Coordinating meetings or follow-up actions

### Core phrases
- *A short alignment meeting may be useful before final issue.*
- *Please let me know your availability for a 30-minute review session.*
- *Once comments are consolidated, I will prepare the next revision.*
- *I suggest closing the editorial points by email and reserving the technical issues for the next meeting.*

## 8. Closing professionally

### Core phrases
- *Please let me know if further clarification is required.*
- *I remain available for any technical questions arising from the review.*
- *Thank you in advance for your support.*
- *Kind regards,*
- *Best regards,*
- *Please advise if you would prefer a short meeting to discuss the open items.*

## 9. Email paragraph models

### Model A: review request
*Please find attached revision 3 of the interface note for your review. The current update reflects the comments received during the design meeting, with particular changes in Sections 4 and 6. Please confirm by Thursday whether the revised acceptance wording is suitable for internal issue.*

### Model B: technical status update
*This message provides a short update on the current verification status. The latest continuity test did not reproduce the anomaly observed during the previous cycle. However, the issue remains open pending environmental retest. Release to service is therefore not recommended at this stage.*

### Model C: clarification request
*I am writing to request clarification regarding the requirement statement in Section 5. As currently written, the sentence appears to combine a mandatory condition with a recommendation. Please confirm whether the intended wording should remain prescriptive or be reclassified as guidance.*

## 10. Weak vs strong email wording

### Weak
*Hi, I send you the document. Please check and tell me if is okay.*

### Stronger
*Please find attached the updated document for your review. The main changes affect the interface description and the associated acceptance wording. Please confirm by Friday whether the current revision is acceptable for internal issue.*

### Weak
*There are some problems in the report and maybe we need a meeting.*

### Stronger
*Two points remain open in the report: the traceability statement in Section 3 and the acceptance criterion in Section 6. A short review meeting may therefore be useful before release.*

## 11. Email review checklist

- Is the purpose explicit in the first sentence?
- Is the required action clear?
- Is the level of urgency proportionate?
- Is the document or revision identified precisely?
- Is the closing appropriate for the relationship and context?

## Final note

!!! note "A good technical email reduces follow-up traffic"
    The best technical emails make it easy for the recipient to understand the context, identify the expected action, and respond without asking for basic clarification.
