    Phrasebank for Meetings¶

    Purpose of this page¶

    Engineering meetings often fail linguistically not because participants lack technical knowledge, but because they lack controlled phrases for framing scope, clarifying assumptions, recording decisions, and assigning actions. A meeting that is technically rich but linguistically vague creates weak minutes, unclear responsibilities, and avoidable follow-up work.

    This phrasebank supports participation in design reviews, integration meetings, technical coordination sessions, maintenance discussions, and document-review meetings.

    1. Opening and framing the meeting¶

    Core phrases¶

    • The purpose of todayâ€™s review is to...
    • This meeting is intended to confirm...
    • The main open point concerns...
    • The discussion is limited to the current configuration baseline.
    • Todayâ€™s session focuses on interface allocation and closure criteria.
    • The objective is to align on technical content before document release.

    Expanded example¶

    The purpose of todayâ€™s review is to confirm whether the revised interface arrangement is acceptable for the current baseline and to identify any remaining issues that may affect document release.

    2. Introducing an issue or concern¶

    Core phrases¶

    • The main concern relates to...
    • An open issue remains regarding...
    • The current wording may be unclear in relation to...
    • We need to distinguish between the observed issue and the proposed corrective action.
    • The available evidence does not yet support closure of this point.

    3. Asking for clarification¶

    Core phrases¶

    • Could you clarify whether this point affects the external interface?
    • Do we have confirmed data for that assumption?
    • Can we distinguish between the observed issue and the proposed corrective action?
    • Is this a design preference or a formal requirement?
    • Does this apply to all configurations or only to the tested unit?
    • Can you confirm whether the current drawing issue is the latest approved version?

    Usage note¶

    Questions in technical meetings should reduce ambiguity, not increase it. A strong question usually identifies the exact uncertainty.

    4. Expressing agreement carefully¶

    Core phrases¶

    • I agree with that interpretation.
    • That approach appears consistent with the current requirement set.
    • The proposed wording is acceptable from a technical perspective.
    • I support the update, provided that the limitation is stated explicitly.
    • That seems reasonable for the current review stage.

    5. Expressing disagreement professionally¶

    Core phrases¶

    • I am not sure the current evidence supports that conclusion.
    • I would be cautious about approving that wording at this stage.
    • That interpretation may be too broad for the available data.
    • The proposed text does not clearly separate requirement from recommendation.
    • I do not think this point is sufficiently closed for release.

    Usage note¶

    Professional disagreement should focus on evidence, requirement status, or document logic, not on personal authority.

    6. Requesting action during the meeting¶

    Core phrases¶

    • Please update Section 4 to reflect the revised connector allocation.
    • Could the integration team provide dimensional confirmation by next Wednesday?
    • Please circulate the revised draft after incorporating todayâ€™s comments.
    • Quality should review the acceptance wording before external issue.
    • Can maintenance confirm whether the current access arrangement is workable in service conditions?

    7. Recording decisions¶

    Core phrases¶

    • The team agreed to retain the current arrangement pending further verification.
    • No change was approved at this stage.
    • Action 03 remains open until dimensional confirmation is received.
    • The review confirmed provisional acceptance subject to closure of the listed comments.
    • The current wording was accepted for internal issue only.
    • Release remains on hold pending completion of the agreed updates.

    8. Summarising status at the end of the meeting¶

    Core phrases¶

    • To summarise, three points remain open.
    • The main outcome of todayâ€™s review is that the design is acceptable in principle, subject to minor updates.
    • The document can proceed to the next review stage once the open actions are closed.
    • The technical issue is considered understood, but the verification evidence is not yet complete.
    • We have alignment on the design intent, but not yet on the final requirement wording.

    9. Action and responsibility language¶

    Core phrases¶

    • The integration team will provide the updated dimensions.
    • Quality will review the compliance wording.
    • The author will issue revision 3 by Friday.
    • Maintenance will confirm service access constraints.
    • Systems engineering will review the interface statement against the baseline requirement set.

    10. Chairing or facilitating language¶

    Core phrases¶

    • Let us return to the original question.
    • Before moving on, we need to close the point on...
    • Can we separate the design issue from the documentation issue?
    • For clarity, I will summarise the agreed position.
    • Let us record that as an open action rather than a closed decision.

    11. Weak vs strong meeting language¶

    Weak¶

    So I think maybe this is okay, but there are some issues and someone should check it.

    Stronger¶

    The current arrangement appears technically acceptable; however, two review points remain open. Maintenance should confirm access feasibility, and quality should review the acceptance wording before document release.

    Weak¶

    We agree more or less to keep this solution.

    Stronger¶

    The team agreed to retain the current solution for the baseline configuration, subject to closure of the dimensional verification point.

    12. Mini meeting-summary model¶

    The purpose of the review was to confirm the acceptability of the revised connector layout. The team agreed that the arrangement is technically viable for the current baseline. However, two points remain open: final dimensional confirmation and review of the associated maintenance note. Revision 3 will be issued after closure of both actions.

    Final note¶

    Meeting language should create traceability

    A technically effective meeting is one in which the language used makes scope, evidence, disagreement, action, and decision status visible enough to be recorded accurately afterwards.
